name story
The phrase 浩然之气 (hào rán zhī qì) first appeared in the Mencius, written around 300 BCE. Mencius described it as a vast, righteous energy that fills the universe — the moral force that arises when a person lives in complete alignment with their values. The poet Meng Haoran, who lived during the Tang dynasty, carried this energy in his very name. He was known for turning down a government post to spend his life writing about mountains, rivers, and solitude. His poetry is still memorized by Chinese schoolchildren today.
